Classical Dance Forms of India

Bhangra

Bhangra is a vibrant and energetic folk dance from the state of Punjab, in northern India and Pakistan. Originally, it was performed by farmers to celebrate the harvest season, especially the festival of Vaisakhi, which marks the Punjabi New Year and the wheat harvest.

Bhangra is a lively and energetic folk dance that originated in the Punjab region of India and Pakistan. Traditionally, it was performed by farmers to celebrate the harvest season, especially during the festival of Vaisakhi. Over time, Bhangra evolved into a popular form of celebration at weddings, festivals, and cultural events. The dance is known for its high-energy moves, strong footwork, and colorful costumes. It is usually accompanied by the dhol, a powerful double-headed drum, along with other traditional instruments. Today, Bhangra is performed around the world and has even blended with modern music styles like hip-hop and pop, making it a global symbol of joy, celebration, and Punjabi culture.

History of Bhangra

Bhangra is a lively and energetic folk dance that originated in the Punjab region of India and Pakistan. It began as a celebratory dance performed by farmers during the harvest festival of Vaisakhi, which marks the beginning of the Punjabi New Year and the harvesting of wheat. Traditionally performed by men in the fields, Bhangra was a way to express joy and gratitude for a good harvest. Over time, different villages developed their own variations of the dance, and it evolved into a more structured form. In the mid-20th century, Bhangra was adapted for stage performances and became popular in cultural festivals and competitions. With the rise of the Punjabi diaspora, especially in the UK and Canada, Bhangra gained global popularity and began fusing with Western music styles like hip-hop and pop, creating a modern, dynamic version of the traditional dance. Today, Bhangra remains a symbol of Punjabi pride, energy, and cultural identity around the world.

Music and Instruments

Bhangra is always performed with live or recorded folk music, typically featuring:
Dhol – A loud, upbeat drum that sets the rhythm.
Chimta – Metal tongs with bells.
Tumbi – A small, high-pitched string instrument.
Algoza – Double-flute instrument.
Dafli – A type of tambourine.

Costume

Bhangra dancers wear bright, colorful clothing that reflects Punjabi tradition:
Men wear turbans (pagdi), kurtas, lungis, and waistcoats.
Women (in Giddha – the female counterpart to Bhangra) wear salwar kameez with dupattas and jewelry.
